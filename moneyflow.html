Money Flow
  • Users vote using Rakuten horse racing.
  • The organizer refunds 75% of the total bet to the user.
  • The organizer pays Rakuten 8%
  • Rakuten pays 4.5% of the total premium to the SPAT management (and racetrack management organization) as a system usage fee.
  • The organizer pays the prize money to the owner, jockey and other related parties.

The estimated prize money for first place is 100 to 300 million yen for large races at central horse racing, 100 to 50 million yen for graded regional horse racing, and several million yen for regular races.

2nd to 5th places will also receive prize money, but the amount of money received will be about half the amount for each position.

Horse Racing Characters and Money Flow
Characters Involved

This is the so-called “user” who purchases the betting ticket.

Race organizer. Securing and announcing horses to run and racecourses.

I am the owner of the racetrack. As the name suggests, we manage and rent racetracks. In addition, the organization that manages the four South Kanto racetracks also operates a race betting system called SPAT for the South Kanto racetracks + local racetracks. Rakuten horse racing is connected to this SPAT to purchase betting tickets etc.

We belong to Rakuten horse racing. We provide a UI that allows you to vote for races held at South Kanto and local racetracks via SPAT.

This includes the owners and jockeys of the horses that actually run in the race, and the personnel who care for the horses.
