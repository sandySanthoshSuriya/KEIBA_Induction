What is Horse Racing?

Horse racing is a competition in which jockeys ride horses(thoroughbreds) and run a distance (1000m to 3600m) on a predetermined course (turf, dirt, obstacles) to compete for the fastest speed, and to predict the order of finish.

Race horses run at speeds of 60 to 70 kilometers per hour, so the sense of speed gives us a sense of excitement.

Horse Racing History

At that time, there was no money betting, but over time, from around 1500 AD, European kings and aristocrats began to hold horse races.

As a result, gambling began to take place among the nobility first, and later among the commoners, and as a result, it became an entertainment with two aspects of sports and gambling, which is familiar to the general public, like today's horse racing.

Horse Racing types

There are two types of horse racing with different organizers.

  • Central horse racing :

    • Sponsored by the Japan Racing Association (JRA)
    All races held at 10 racetracks across Japan (Hakodate, Sapporo, Fukushima, Niigata, Nakayama, Tokyo, Chukyo, Kyoto, Hanshin and Kokura) are managed by the JRA.

  • Regional horse racing :

    • Sponsored by local governments such as prefectures and municipalities.
    Local horse racing is horse racing run by local governments such as prefectures and municipalities . There are 15 local horse racing organizers and 18 racetracks nationwide .

Flat race : A racehorse runs a set distance from the starting point of the course. Compete for speed. Basically, almost all horse racing in Japan follows this format.

Banei Race : A racehorse runs an obstacle course while pulling a heavy sled. Compete for power and speed. Held only at Obihiro Racecourse

Night horse racing : Horse racing is held on weekday nights, so you can enjoy horse racing after work .
